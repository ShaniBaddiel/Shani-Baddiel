Crowdfunding for South Africa



South Africa is struggling with a large issue at hand. Unemployment.

How can we help the crisis that is affecting millions of people every day, in South Africa?

While people in the developed countries are able to afford comfortable housing, central heating in the cold weather, food, clothes, medical aid and education, African children are often found on the streets begging. The government has given portions of land for these people to live on, yet houses consist of a four by four meter tinned shack, no electricity or running water. There are "toiletsï¿½ situated outside their homes. Their living areas are overly populated and disease spreads quickly. Medical aid is extremely costly therefore these people would rather wait hours on end at a government hospital, waiting to be received. Yet correct medical aid is not always correctly prescribed. If these youth would only be able to have the funding to provide them with their education they need, they will become self-efficient, and live comfortable lives on a much higher standard. And this is where crowdfunding comes in.

By crowdfunding, one can raise large amounts of money in order to pay for young South Africans to acquire an education. There are so many youth who cannot afford to pay for a tertiary education, which leads to one of the biggest reasons for unemployment. Businesses and companies are only interested in employing those who have received degrees in certain fields.

This leads to the sight we see so often of people begging, everywhere. Next to shopping centres, on main roads and in any other street.

"I feel that they are desperate and in pain, regardless of their individual circumstances. A person who needs to beg in order to support themselves is obviously in a painful situation. Even a person who already has their basic needs met and has simply found a way to make extra money by preying on other people is clearly suffering, at least in my eyes. Their entitlement suggests an anger that probably arises from a deep hurt.

I feel deeply ashamed. No civilised society should have people on the streets. It's a failure at the most basic level. I try not to feel bad when I can't offer anything, but when I am able to, I don't waste time worrying about where my small contribution will actually go. If someone is begging on the street, they are desperate in their own way."
( By Sarah Rouan)
Many people do not have a job. Instead, they walk around in the street, unemployed and desperate.

If we were to take true pity on these people and aspire to help them, the best thing to do would be to give them a job.

By raising an enormous sum of money through crowdfunding, we could use it to pay towards tuition fees and tertiary education. Our people would no longer need to feel dejected. They will be able to walk proudly, go to work every day and be purposeful. South Africa will then become an upper-class country, a land in which all are dignified and respected. No one will have to resort to means of begging, any longer. Rather, the future of South Africa will become brighter, and ultimately, through crowdfunding, the problem of unemployment could potentially disappear. Never to return again.
